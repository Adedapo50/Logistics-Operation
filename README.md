# Logistics-Operation
This dataset captures operational, financial, fleet management, safety, and customer-related information associated with a logistics business. It integrates data from multiple business functions to support decision-making through interactive dashboards and performance monitoring.
The dataset includes information on loads and shipments, such as revenue generated, booking types, load categories, freight weight, fuel surcharges, and accessorial charges. These records enable the organization to evaluate profitability, revenue trends, and the financial contribution of different shipment types.

It also contains customer-related data, including customer names and customer classifications. This allows for the analysis of customer segments and identification of high-value clients contributing most significantly to revenue.

The routing component of the dataset provides destination city information, supporting geographic analysis of freight movements and helping managers identify the most profitable or frequently serviced routes.

From an operational perspective, the dataset tracks drivers and delivery performance through metrics such as total revenue by driver, active driver counts, on-time delivery percentages, and average detention times. These indicators help assess workforce productivity and service quality.

The fleet management section includes truck utilization metrics, average miles per gallon (MPG), downtime hours, and utilization rates. Such data is essential for optimizing asset usage, reducing idle time, and improving overall fleet efficiency.

Additionally, the dataset records fuel purchase information, including fuel consumption volumes, fuel costs, and fuel-related performance measures such as cost per mile. These metrics assist in controlling operating expenses and monitoring fuel efficiency.

A strong emphasis is also placed on safety and maintenance management. The dataset captures safety incidents, claim amounts, incident types, vehicle damage costs, preventable incident rates, maintenance activities, maintenance costs, and downtime associated with repairs. These insights support risk management initiatives and proactive maintenance planning.

Major Tables Identified
Loads – shipment details, revenue, profit, load type, booking type, weight.
Customers – customer names and customer categories.
Routes – destination cities and route performance.
Drivers – driver information and productivity measures.
Delivery Events – on-time performance and detention metrics.
Truck Utilization Metrics – utilization rates, MPG, and downtime statistics.
Fuel Purchases – fuel usage and cost records.
Maintenance Records – maintenance activities, costs, and repair downtime.
Safety Incidents – incident occurrences, claim values, and damage costs.
Purpose of the Dataset

The primary purpose of this dataset is to provide a 360-degree view of logistics operations, enabling managers and stakeholders to:

Monitor financial performance and profitability.
Evaluate customer and route profitability.
Track driver productivity and delivery efficiency.
Optimize fleet utilization and fuel consumption.
Improve maintenance scheduling and reduce downtime.
Enhance safety performance and minimize operational risks.
